My primary interests are in discrete mathematics, error-correcting codes and distributed data storage. I did my PhD with Prof. Mary Wootters at Stanford, where part of my PhD was funded by a Google Graduate Fellowship in Stanford Engineering. Prior to that, I studied computer science at St John's College at the University of Cambridge, and mathematics at Brown University.

In the past, I was a Senior Software Engineer at Google, where I conducted research and delivered production code on various aspects of data storage technologies.

Outside of Math/CS, I love cooking, playing tennis, and learning about wine.
